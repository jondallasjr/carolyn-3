About This Guide

Understanding the DTV visa and one person's journey to medical tourism in Thailand

Why This Guide Exists

The Thailand DTV (Destination Thailand Visa) is a relatively new 5-year visa designed for digital nomads and people seeking long-term medical treatment. However, official information is scattered, contradictory, and often outdated.

This guide consolidates real-world experience, official requirements, and practical timelines into one comprehensive resource. It's designed to answer the questions most people have when considering a move to Thailand for medical care.

The Author's Story

Carolyn relocated to Chiang Mai, Thailand in August 2025 to pursue long-term physical therapy and medical treatment in a more affordable environment. What began as a personal relocation checklist evolved into a detailed, phase-by-phase guide to obtaining the DTV visa.

The journey from initial research to DTV approval takes approximately 4-5 months and involves:

  • Pre-departure preparation and document gathering
  • Initial 60-day entry to Thailand
  • Establishing a medical plan with Thai doctors
  • Building financial and legal sponsorship documentation
  • A brief trip to Vietnam for the actual visa application
  • Return to Thailand with a 5-year visa

Who This Guide Is For

This resource is intended for:

  • People seeking medical treatment – Physical therapy, surgery recovery, dental work, or other medical care at a fraction of US costs
  • Digital nomads – Remote workers wanting long-term stability in Thailand
  • US citizens – Currently, the DTV is most accessible to US passport holders
  • Those with time to plan – The process requires 4-5 months of preparation

Our Mission

We believe accurate, detailed information should be freely available. The DTV process doesn't have to be confusing or expensive. By sharing a real timeline, actual costs, and honest challenges, this guide aims to help others make informed decisions about medical tourism in Thailand.

Whether you ultimately choose Thailand or not, you deserve clear, practical information to guide that decision.

Important Disclaimer

This guide is informational only and not legal advice. Immigration laws and visa requirements change frequently. Always:

  • Verify current requirements with the Thai Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  • Consult with a Thai immigration lawyer before applying
  • Contact your nearest Thai embassy for official guidance
  • Confirm medical facility requirements directly with hospitals

The timeline and requirements in this guide reflect conditions as of October 2025 and may not apply to future applications.
