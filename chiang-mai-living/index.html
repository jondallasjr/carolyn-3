Chiang Mai Living Guide

Practical information for daily life in Thailand's northern hub

Neighborhoods & Housing

Suthep Area

Large expat community with English-speaking services. Close to Chiang Mai University and hiking.

  • Housing Cost: $250-500/month
  • Best for: Expats, families, quieter setting
  • Amenities: Gyms, pools, restaurants, grocery stores

Old City

Historic temples and traditional Thai culture. Central location with good restaurants.

  • Vibe: Cultural, walkable, touristy
  • Best for: First-time visitors, cultural immersion

Nimman

Modern, trendy area with cafes, bars, and young professionals.

  • Vibe: Youthful, contemporary, nightlife
  • Best for: Digital nomads, social scene

Transportation

Grab App

Download and set up Grab for taxis and motorbikes. Very affordable compared to Western cities.

Public Transport

Buses and songthaews (shared red trucks) are the cheapest local transportation options.

Motorcycle Rental

Affordable option for getting around. International license recommended but not always required.

Walking

Chiang Mai is very walkable, especially in Suthep and Old City areas.

Healthcare & Medical Services

Bangkok Hospital Chiang Mai

24/7 English-speaking facility with proven track record for DTV medical letters.

  • Consultation: ~$40-90
  • Excellent for medical documentation

Physical Therapy

Specialized treatment at fraction of US costs ($25-75/session vs $100+ in US).

  • Multiple clinics available
  • English-speaking therapists

Pharmacies

Many US medications available at lower prices. Bring list of prescriptions for local equivalents.

  • Widely available throughout city
  • Over-the-counter availability varies

Practical Tips

Language

Learn basic Thai to enhance your experience. Many locals appreciate the effort, though English is spoken in expat areas.

Air Quality

Plan for burning season (March-May). Consider southern Thailand or Bali trip during worst air quality months.

Cell Phone

Get a cell phone plan at the airport upon arrival. Very inexpensive with good coverage.

Banking

ATM withdrawals cost 220 THB (~$6.50) per transaction. Plan withdrawals accordingly.

Medication Supply

Research prescription medication availability before arrival. Bring documentation and consider 90-day backup supply.
