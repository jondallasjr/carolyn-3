Tourist Visa Extension (30-Day)

Extend your 60-day tourist visa to 90 days

Overview

If you're entering Thailand on a tourist visa exemption (60 days for US citizens), you can extend your stay by an additional 30 days at the Thai Immigration Office in Chiang Mai. This gives you a total of 90 days to settle in and prepare for your DTV application.

Key Information

When to Apply

Days 20-30 of your initial 60-day stay

Can be applied for anytime before your 60-day visa expires

Processing Time

Same-day processing

Arrive early in the morning for fastest service

Cost

1,900 THB (~$55 USD)

Bring cash; not all immigration offices accept cards

Result

Total stay extended to 90 days

Provides extra time before DTV application deadline

Location & Hours

Chiang Mai Immigration Office

  • Address: Huay Kaew Rd, Chiang Mai 50200
  • Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:30 AM - 4:30 PM
  • Closed: Thai national holidays and weekends

Arrive early (8:30-9:00 AM) for faster processing and less crowding

Required Documents

  • Passport with current 60-day tourist stamp
  • TM.7 Form (available at immigration or download ahead of time)
  • One 4x6 cm passport photo (bring 2 just in case)
  • Cash: 1,900 THB

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Get the form: Pick up TM.7 form at immigration office or print ahead of time
  2. Fill out the form: Complete all fields with your passport information
  3. Submit documents: Give passport, completed form, photo, and fee to officer
  4. Receive stamp: Get your 30-day extension stamp in passport
  5. Total stay: You now have 90 days from original arrival date

⏱️ Timeline Tip

According to your relocation timeline, you should apply for the 30-day extension around October 19-20 (days 25-26 of your stay). This brings your tourist stay to 90 days total, giving you time to:

  • Complete medical consultations
  • Prepare your full DTV application package
  • Travel to Vietnam for DTV processing without rushing

Is This Extension Necessary?

Optional but recommended. You can:

  • Extend to 90 days: Gives you the most time to prepare for DTV (recommended)
  • Skip extension: Border run to Laos/Myanmar at day 60, return with new 60-day stamp
  • Leave at day 60: Go to Vietnam early for DTV processing

For medical tourism on the DTV, having the full 90 days is beneficial for establishing your medical plan with Bangkok Hospital.

Common Tips

  • ✓ Bring more passport photos than needed (always useful to have extras)
  • ✓ Come on a weekday morning for fastest service
  • ✓ Ask for the "30-day extension" clearly if language is a barrier
  • ✓ Keep your extension stamp safe—you'll need it for all official matters
  • ✓ Mark your new departure date on a calendar (90 days from arrival)

Next Steps

After extending your visa to 90 days, focus on:

  1. Schedule your medical consultation at Bangkok Hospital
  2. Begin gathering DTV application documents
  3. Arrange travel to Vietnam (around day 75-80)
  4. Submit your DTV application within 24 hours of arriving in Vietnam
