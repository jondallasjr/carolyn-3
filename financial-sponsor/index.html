Financial Sponsor Guide

Complete requirements, responsibilities, and documentation for DTV financial sponsors

What is a Financial Sponsor?

A financial sponsor is a person who commits to provide financial support for your stay in Thailand under the DTV visa. The sponsor demonstrates financial stability and agrees to cover emergency expenses, such as medical costs or repatriation to your home country.

Note: You can be your own financial sponsor if you meet the financial requirements.

Financial Requirements

Bank Balance

500,000+ THB (~$15,000 USD)

Must be maintained for at least 3+ months before application

Currency

Thai Baht (THB)

Funds must be held in Thai currency in a Thai bank account

Documentation

Bank Statements

Provide 3+ months of consecutive statements showing the balance

Sponsorship Letter Requirements

The financial sponsor must provide a formal letter committing to financial support. The letter should include:

  • Clear statement of commitment to provide financial support
  • Specific commitment to cover emergency expenses (medical, repatriation)
  • Statement that sponsor will pay for return flight to USA if needed
  • Sponsor's full name, address, phone number, and email
  • Date and signature of the sponsor
  • Notarization (recommended for US sponsors)

Important: The letter must explicitly state willingness to cover emergency repatriation costs. Vague or generic letters may result in rejection.

Proof of Relationship

You must provide documentation proving your relationship to the financial sponsor:

  • Immediate Family: Birth certificate, marriage certificate, or adoption papers
  • Extended Family: Family tree documentation or genealogical records
  • Non-Family Sponsors: Character reference letter from employer, organization, or community leader
  • Self-Sponsorship: No relationship documentation needed

Who Can Be a Financial Sponsor?

✓ Yourself (Self-Sponsor)

You can sponsor yourself if you have 500,000+ THB in a Thai bank account. This is the most straightforward option.

✓ Family Member

Parent, sibling, spouse, or adult child with demonstrated financial stability and willingness to support you.

✓ Close Friend or Associate

A person who knows you well and can provide a character reference with financial documentation.

✓ Employer or Organization

Your employer or an organization you're affiliated with (for treatment/medical tourism cases).

✗ Cannot Sponsor You

Individuals with criminal records, immigration violations, or those who cannot provide verifiable financial documentation.

Sponsor Responsibilities

  • Financial Commitment: Maintain 500,000+ THB throughout your visa period
  • Emergency Support: Be prepared to cover unexpected medical or repatriation costs
  • Documentation: Provide accurate and truthful financial information
  • Availability: Respond to Thai immigration inquiries if contacted
  • Compliance: Ensure all information remains accurate and updated

Duration: Sponsorship responsibilities typically last for the duration of your DTV visa (5 years) or until you establish your own financial independence in Thailand.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I be my own financial sponsor?

Yes. If you have 500,000+ THB in a Thai bank account, you can self-sponsor. You'll need bank statements showing the balance for 3+ months, and you'll sign your own sponsorship commitment letter.

What if my sponsor lives outside Thailand?

The sponsor's funds must be in a Thai bank account. If your sponsor lives abroad, they'll need to transfer funds to Thailand or open a Thai account. The financial commitment is what matters, not the sponsor's residence.

Can the sponsor's funds be in USD or another currency?

No. The 500,000 THB requirement must be in Thai Baht in a Thai bank account. Exchange rates apply if converting from another currency.

What happens to the sponsored funds if my visa is approved?

The funds remain in the sponsor's account. The Thai government doesn't confiscate them—they simply verify that the funds exist and can be accessed for emergencies. The money is yours (or your sponsor's) to use normally.

Can I change sponsors after my visa is approved?

Possibly, but it's complex and may require immigration approval. Choose your sponsor carefully and ensure they can commit for the long term.

What if my sponsor can't maintain the 500,000 THB balance?

Immigration may investigate and potentially revoke your visa. It's critical that the sponsor maintains the required balance throughout your visa period.

Important Reminders

  • All documents must be error-free; typos can cause automatic rejection
  • Bank statements must clearly show the account holder's name and balance
  • The sponsorship letter must be signed and dated by the sponsor
  • If the sponsor is not fluent in English, have their documents professionally translated
  • Keep copies of all sponsorship documents for your records
  • Verify all information with Thai embassy requirements before submitting

Next Steps

Once you have identified your financial sponsor and gathered documentation:

  1. Review all documents for accuracy and completeness
  2. Have documents professionally translated if needed
  3. Contact your nearest Thai embassy for final verification
  4. Proceed with DTV Application when ready
