Finding Housing in Chiang Mai

A practical guide to locating, evaluating, and securing accommodation in Chiang Mai, with focus on the Suthep neighborhood.

Housing Overview

Chiang Mai offers excellent value for housing, with quality accommodations significantly cheaper than Western countries. Whether you're looking for a temporary base or permanent residence, this guide covers the process from search to move-in.

Quick Facts

  • Suthep Area Range: $250–$500/month for furnished apartments
  • Lease Terms: Monthly to yearly (yearly offers 10–20% discount)
  • Deposits: Typically 1–2 months rent
  • Best Neighborhoods for Expats: Suthep, Nimman, Old City, Chiang Mai University area

Where to Search

Online Platforms

  • Airbnb: Short-term, good for first 2–4 weeks while apartment hunting
  • Booking.com: Monthly deals available; contact directly for rates
  • Thai Property Sites: DDproperty.com, Doonung.com (Thai-language but searchable)
  • Facebook Groups: "Chiang Mai Apartments," "Accommodation in Chiang Mai," "Housing in Chiang Mai"
  • Nomad Lists: Community forums with vetted recommendations

On-the-Ground Search

  • Walk around your target neighborhood and look for "For Rent" signs (สำหรับให้เช่า)
  • Ask local residents and other expats for recommendations
  • Visit local real estate agents (many English-speaking)
  • Check community boards at cafes and coworking spaces

Suthep Neighborhood Guide

Suthep (สุเทพ) is highly recommended for expats seeking community, amenities, and proximity to healthcare.

Why Choose Suthep?

  • Large, established expat community
  • Close to Chiang Mai University (cultural events, markets)
  • Multiple gyms, pools, and recreation centers
  • Restaurants serving international and Thai cuisine
  • Bangkok Hospital and other medical facilities nearby
  • Access to hiking and nature activities
  • Active AA meetings and recovery communities

Nearby Amenities in Suthep

Healthcare

Bangkok Hospital Chiang Mai, Health Care Medical Clinic, multiple pharmacies

Shopping & Food

Lotus's supermarket, local markets, numerous restaurants

Recreation

Gyms, swimming pools, yoga studios, art centers

Community

AA meetings (50+ per week in Chiang Mai), expat groups, co-working spaces

Housing Search Checklist

What to Look For in an Apartment

Essential

  • Air conditioning (crucial for comfort and health)
  • Hot water and adequate water pressure
  • Reliable WiFi (test before signing)
  • Secure door locks and windows
  • Natural light and ventilation

Highly Desirable

  • Furnished (saves on setup costs)
  • Quiet, away from main roads
  • Proximity to markets, restaurants, medical facilities
  • Gym or pool access (building or nearby)
  • Cleaning service available or included

Red Flags

  • Landlord unwilling to provide written lease
  • Excessive upfront costs (more than deposit + first month)
  • Mold, dampness, or pest evidence
  • No emergency contact or maintenance response system
  • Unclear terms about utilities and additional fees

Cost Breakdown

Item Monthly Cost (USD) Notes
Furnished 1BR apartment (Suthep) $250–$500 Yearly lease offers 10–20% discount
Utilities (electricity, water, WiFi) $30–$60 Varies by usage; WiFi often included
Cleaning service (2–3 hrs/week) $15–$30 500–800 THB per session
Deposit (typical) $250–$500 Refundable at lease end (minus damages)

Negotiation Tips

  • Lease Length: Offer 12-month commitment for 15–20% discount
  • Utilities: Ask which utilities are included; negotiate if not
  • Deposit: Negotiate down to 1 month if possible (standard is 1–2 months)
  • Furnishings: If unfurnished, ask if landlord will provide basics (bed, refrigerator)
  • Cancellation: Seek 30-day cancellation clause with deposit forfeit (vs. full lease liability)
  • Walk Around: Document existing damage before move-in; get landlord signature

Alternatives to Traditional Lease

Coliving Spaces

Increasingly popular in Chiang Mai; include furniture, utilities, and community amenities. Prices: $400–$800/month. Good for short-term adjustment.

Serviced Apartments

Hotel-style apartments with cleaning, WiFi, 24/7 reception. Prices: $500–$1,200/month. Premium option for first 1–3 months.

Monthly Airbnb/Booking.com

Flexible for first month while apartment hunting. Book in advance for discounts. Transition to annual lease once settled.

Moving Timeline

Pre-Arrival (3–4 weeks before): Search online, book temporary accommodation, bookmark listings.

Arrival Week 1: Settle into Airbnb, explore Suthep, contact promising landlords via Facebook.

Week 2–3: Tour apartments, negotiate terms, finalize lease.

End of Week 3: Move into permanent housing.
