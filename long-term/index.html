Long-Term Stay (5+ Years)

Managing your status after DTV approval: staying continuously for up to 5 years.

Overview

Once you've successfully obtained your 5-year DTV visa, managing your long-term stay in Thailand is straightforward. The DTV allows you to remain in Thailand for extended periods with minimal visa maintenance.

Continuous Stay Options

Option 1: 360-Day Continuous Stay

You can stay in Thailand continuously for up to 360 days (nearly a full year) without leaving.

  • Valid for full 360 days from your entry date
  • No re-entry permit required with DTV
  • After 360 days, you must leave Thailand briefly and re-enter
  • Upon re-entry, you get a fresh 180-day stamp

Option 2: Extension Before 180 Days

If you prefer to stay longer in Thailand continuously, extend before your initial 180 days expire.

  • Extension cost: approximately $300 USD (9,900 THB)
  • Processing: Same-day or next-day at Immigration office
  • Extends your stay to a total of 360 days
  • Must apply before your current permission expires

Option 3: Border Runs Every 180 Days

The most flexible and cost-effective approach for many long-term residents.

  • Leave Thailand and immediately re-enter every 180 days
  • Each re-entry grants a fresh 180-day stamp
  • Popular border runs: Laos (Vientiane), Cambodia (Poipet), Malaysia
  • Cost: $50-150 USD depending on destination
  • Time: Can be done in a weekend trip

Key Points for DTV Holders

  • No Re-entry Permits Needed: Unlike other visas, DTV holders don't need separate re-entry permits for border runs
  • 5-Year Validity: Your DTV visa is valid for 5 years, meaning you don't need to reapply for visa approval
  • Each Entry = New Stamp: Each time you re-enter Thailand, immigration stamps your passport with a new entry permission (180 days)
  • Medical/Wellness Focus: Remember to maintain your original purpose (medical treatment, wellness, digital nomad work) in case of inquiries
  • Financial Requirements: While there's no ongoing financial requirement after approval, maintaining stability is prudent

Recommended Timeline Strategy

Months 1-6

Settle into your chosen location (e.g., Chiang Mai). Establish routines, build community, access medical/wellness services as planned.

Month 5-6

Decide your strategy: extension, border run, or partial stay. Plan your next movement.

Month 6

If extending: Visit Immigration to extend for another 180 days. If border running: Book travel for your trip.

Years 1-5

Repeat your chosen maintenance method. Your 5-year DTV remains valid throughout.

Budget Considerations

Item Cost Frequency
Extension (Optional) ~$300 USD Every 180-360 days
Border Run (Alternative) $50-150 USD Every 180-360 days
DTV Renewal (After 5 Years) $340 USD (application fee) Once after 5 years
Living Expenses (Chiang Mai) $800-1,500 USD/month Monthly

Important Reminders

After 5 Years: Your DTV expires and you'll need to reapply if you wish to continue. The process mirrors your initial application: new medical plan, sponsor documentation, and approval through a Thai embassy.

Passport Space: With frequent stamps, monitor your passport's remaining pages. You can get passport pages added or renew your passport as needed.

Keep Records: Save copies of all DTV approvals, medical letters, and financial sponsor documentation for future applications or inquiries.
