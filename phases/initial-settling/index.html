Phase 3: Initial Settling In

Your first 3 weeks in Chiang Mai: recovery, setting up essential services, and connecting with community

Welcome to Chiang Mai

Your first three weeks are about finding your rhythm, establishing a support system, and taking care of practical necessities. This is a quieter phase focused on rest, recovery, and building connections.

Dates: August 26 - September 16, 2025
Duration: 3 weeks

What You'll Do During Initial Settling

First 3 Days: Rest & Recover

Take it easy after your 25+ hour journey. Adjust to the time zone, rest, and acclimate to your new environment.

  • Sleep and recover from travel
  • Visit nearby grocery store
  • Download and set up Grab app for transportation
üè•

Set Up Basic Services

Establish your healthcare and support infrastructure immediately for continuity of care.

  • Schedule appointment with primary care doctor (recommended: Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic)
  • Find and book physical therapist for ongoing treatment
  • Locate nearby pharmacy and fill prescriptions
  • Arrange cleaning service (500-800 THB / $15-24 for 2-3 hours)
ü§ù

Connect with Recovery Community

Building your support network is crucial for long-term success and wellbeing in Thailand.

  • Attend nearby AA meetings (Chiang Mai has 50+ meetings/week)
  • Explore Buddhist recovery and Refuge Recovery meetings
  • Find a sponsor
  • Research addiction counseling options (Counselling Thailand, The Cabin alumni network)
üìç

Get Oriented to Suthep Area

Familiarize yourself with your neighborhood and nearby amenities in the Suthep expat community.

  • Locate convenience stores and grocery stores
  • Explore restaurants and local dining options
  • Map out transportation routes via Grab
  • Identify western-style services and familiar comforts

After Initial Settling

Once you've completed the first 3 weeks and feel settled, you'll move into Phase 4: Deeper Settling In, where you'll expand your activities, join community groups beyond recovery (yoga, hiking, art, cooking), and begin preparing for your DTV medical documentation.
