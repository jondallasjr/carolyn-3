Phase 4: Medical Planning for DTV

Weeks 4-8: Establishing your medical plan with Thai doctors, obtaining treatment letters, and preparing DTV application documents

Phase Overview

This critical phase transforms your medical needs into the foundation for your 5-year DTV visa. You'll establish a formal medical plan with Thai doctors, gather required treatment letters, explore lifestyle activities in Chiang Mai, and compile all documentation needed for your DTV application.

Start: September 16, 2025 End: October 14, 2025 Duration: 4 weeks

Step 1: Get a Medical Plan (Weeks 1-3)

The medical plan is the cornerstone of your DTV application. You need documented proof that you require ongoing medical treatment in Thailand for at least 6 months.

Key Tasks:

  • Contact Bangkok Hospital in Chiang Mai
  • Schedule in-person consultation (~$40-90)
  • Review medical records with Thai doctor
  • Get a 6+ month medical plan for ongoing PT/pain management
  • Receive medical letter documenting treatment justification

Why This Matters

This consultation creates the foundation for visa approval. Bangkok Hospital has a proven track record for DTV medical letters. You must show 6+ months of treatment needed. Physical therapy costs $25-75/session vs $100+ in the US, making it a legitimate justification for your visa.

Step 2: Explore Lifestyle & Community Integration (Ongoing)

Integrate into Chiang Mai's expat and local community while pursuing activities that support your overall wellness and recovery.

Activities to Explore:

  • Get a gym/pool membership
  • Join expat community groups beyond recovery:
  • Yoga classes
  • Hiking groups
  • Art communities
  • Cooking classes
  • Crafting workshops
  • Board game meetups
  • Research volunteering opportunities (teaching English, women's organizations)
  • Take Thai language lessons
  • Attend Buddhist recovery/Refuge Recovery meetings

Community Benefits

Chiang Mai hosts 50+ AA meetings per week and has a large, welcoming expat community with English-speaking services. Engaging in diverse activities strengthens your recovery and demonstrates genuine settlement plans to immigration officials.

Step 3: Prepare DTV Application Documents (Weeks 3-4)

Begin assembling the complete document package required for your DTV application. Accuracy is critical—even minor typos can cause automatic rejection.

Documents to Compile:

  • Medical Documentation:
    • Medical plan and letters from Bangkok Hospital
    • Evidence of 6+ months ongoing treatment needed
  • Financial Sponsor Requirements:
    • Bank statements showing 500,000+ THB ($15,000+ USD) for 3+ months
    • Sponsorship letter committing to financial support (for emergency repatriation)
    • Proof of relationship (birth certificate)
  • Personal Documents:
    • Passport photo (4x6)
    • Valid passport (copy)
    • Treatment plan summary

Critical Requirement

All documents must be error-free. Typos, inconsistencies, or missing information cause automatic rejection. Review everything multiple times before submission.

Optional: 30-Day Tourist Extension

If you need extra time before applying for the DTV, you can extend your initial 60-day tourist visa by 30 days.

Extension Process (Week 4):

  • Visit immigration office in Chiang Mai
  • Pay 1,900 THB fee
  • Bring passport and completed TM.7 form
  • Same-day processing
  • Brings total tourist stay to 90 days (until November 23)

When to Extend

This provides extra time if you need more than 8 weeks to complete your medical plan, gather documents, or wait for your Financial Sponsor to arrange bank statements. Not mandatory, but good insurance.

Financial Overview for Phase 4

Doctor Consultation: $40-90
Medical Plan (6 months): $300-800
Tourist Extension (optional): ~$58 (1,900 THB)
Living Expenses: $750-1,200
Phase 4 Total: ~$1,200-2,200
