Phase 1: Pre-Departure Preparation

7-8 weeks before departure: Essential preparation before leaving the US

Overview

These 7-8 weeks are critical for gathering documents, organizing your life in the US, and laying the groundwork for your Thailand move. This phase spans from approximately 8 weeks before departure through your final packing and airport arrival.

Key Timeline Milestones

Weeks 1-2: Essential Travel Planning

  • Book temporary accommodation for first 2 weeks in Chiang Mai
  • Arrange travel health insurance that covers your move to Thailand

Weeks 1-4: Gather Critical Documents

  • Collect all US medical records (surgeries, MRI, X-rays, etc.) - these are essential for DTV later
  • Obtain and organize passport
  • Create emergency contact list (doctor, hospital, family, sponsor)

Weeks 2-6: Manage Your US Life

  • Sell, donate, or pack possessions
  • Sell or arrange storage for your car
  • Research prescription medication availability in Thailand
  • Get prescriptions refilled (aim for 90-day supply as backup)

Weeks 3-8: Research & Plan

  • Join Chiang Mai Expats Facebook groups
  • Research financial sponsor responsibilities
  • Create Thailand budget based on actual costs vs US disability income
  • Identify 24/7 English-speaking medical facilities
  • Plan for burning season (potential southern Thailand/Bali trip)
  • Locate amenities in Suthep area: convenience stores, groceries, restaurants, AA meetings, counseling centers, gyms, physical therapists, hospitals

Week 8: Final Packing & Check

  • Get prescriptions refilled with proper medical documentation
  • Verify all documents are organized and photocopied
  • Confirm travel arrangements
  • Notify banks and insurance of travel

Important Notes

  • Visa: US citizens get a 60-day visa exemption automatically - no visa required to enter Thailand
  • Medical Records: These are essential for your DTV application later. Do not skip this step
  • Medications: Many US medications are available in Thailand at a fraction of US costs, but bring documentation
  • Location Context: Suthep area in Chiang Mai has a large expat community with many English-speaking services
  • Financial Sponsor: You'll need this for DTV later - make sure your sponsor understands the requirements

Budget Reference

  • Travel health insurance: varies by provider
  • Flight: typical international round-trip ~$800-1200
  • Temporary accommodation (2 weeks): $200-400 depending on location
  • Medical records reproduction: $50-100
  • Miscellaneous shipping/logistics: budget $200-300

Next Steps

Once you complete the pre-departure phase and arrive in Chiang Mai on August 25-26, you'll move into Phase 2: Initial Settling In, where you'll establish local services, connect with the recovery community, and begin building your life in Thailand.
