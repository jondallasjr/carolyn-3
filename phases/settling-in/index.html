Phase 3: Initial Settling In

Your first 2-3 weeks in Chiang Mai are critical for establishing healthcare, connecting with recovery support, setting up daily services, and building your expat community network.

Timeline: August 26 - September 16

First 3 quiet days to settle, then begin establishing services and community connections.

🏥

Healthcare Setup

  • Schedule consultation with primary care doctor (recommend: Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic)
  • Find and book physical therapist for ongoing treatment
  • Locate and register at your pharmacy
  • Get Thai health insurance quote

Cost: Initial consultation ~$40-90

🤝

Recovery Community

  • Attend AA meetings (50+ meetings/week in Chiang Mai)
  • Buddhist recovery/Refuge Recovery meetings
  • Find a sponsor
  • Research addiction counseling options (Counselling Thailand, The Cabin alumni network)

Benefit: Strong recovery infrastructure already in place

🛒

Daily Services

  • Visit nearby grocery store and familiarize yourself with the area
  • Download and set up Grab app for transportation (taxis & motorbikes)
  • Arrange cleaning service (500-800 THB = $15-24 for 2-3 hours)
  • Explore Western-style and Thai restaurants in Suthep area

Advantage: Grab is very inexpensive and reliable

👥

Expat Community

  • Join Chiang Mai Expats Facebook groups
  • Connect with recovery community groups beyond just recovery meetings
  • Explore interest-based groups (yoga, hiking, art, cooking, crafting, board games)
  • Research volunteering opportunities (teaching English, women's organizations)

Bonus: Suthep area has large established expat community

🏠

Housing Considerations

  • Settle into temporary accommodation (first 2 weeks planned)
  • Research permanent housing for future phases
  • Typical housing cost in Suthep: $250-500/month
  • Consider proximity to amenities, healthcare, and community

Note: Take time to explore before committing long-term

📚

Language & Culture

  • Begin learning basic Thai language
  • Familiarize yourself with Thai customs and etiquette
  • Download translation apps for daily communication
  • Connect with locals through community activities

Tip: English is widely spoken in Chiang Mai expat areas, but Thai basics are appreciated

First Week Checklist

Days 1-3: Rest & Orientation

  • Rest and recover from jet lag
  • Go to nearby grocery store
  • Download Grab app and explore transportation
  • Get a local SIM card from airport (if not done)

Days 4-7: Services Setup

  • Contact Bangkok Hospital for consultation scheduling
  • Research and book cleaning service
  • Find and visit nearby physical therapy clinic
  • Locate pharmacy and verify medication availability

Days 8-14: Community Connection

  • Attend first AA meeting
  • Join Chiang Mai Expats Facebook groups
  • Explore at least 2-3 community interest groups
  • Schedule initial doctor consultation

Key Tips for Success

✓ Be Patient with Yourself

Jet lag, culture shock, and adjustment take time. The first 3 quiet days are intentional—use them to rest.

✓ Prioritize Recovery First

Finding your recovery community and support network is foundational to your entire stay. Make this a priority in week 1.

✓ Healthcare Documentation

Schedule your medical consultation early. This is the foundation for your medical plan needed for the DTV application.

✓ Build Your Support Network

Don't isolate. Chiang Mai has a welcoming expat and recovery community. Actively join groups and attend meetings.

✓ Budget Awareness

ATM fees are 220 THB ($6.50) per withdrawal. Consider larger, less frequent withdrawals. Grab taxis are very affordable.

✓ Explore the Suthep Area

Use this settling phase to thoroughly explore your neighborhood and identify all nearby amenities you'll need long-term.
