Phase 2: Travel to Thailand

Flight logistics, visa exemption entry, and your first 24 hours in Chiang Mai. This phase covers your journey from the US to Thailand and initial arrival setup.

Flight: Baltimore → Chiang Mai

Itinerary

  • Route: Baltimore (BWI) → Detroit → Seoul → Chiang Mai
  • Total Journey: 25+ hours with layovers
  • Arrival: Chiang Mai 9:55 PM on August 25
  • Entry: 60-day visa exemption (no visa required for US citizens)

⚠️ Important Notes

  • Airlines may ask for return or onward ticket proof
  • You'll automatically receive a 60-day entry stamp
  • No advance visa application needed
  • Keep your passport and arrival documents safe

Immigration & Arrival (First 24 Hours)

At Immigration

  • Present passport to immigration officer
  • Complete arrival card (TM.6 form provided on plane)
  • Get 60-day tourist entry stamp
  • Keep the departure card attached to your passport

Immediate Tasks

  • Get a local cell phone plan at the airport
    • AIS, Dtac, or True Move are main providers
    • Tourist SIM cards available at airport booths
    • Budget: 300-500 THB ($9-15) for starter package
  • Exchange currency or withdraw Thai Baht from ATM
    • ATM fee: 220 THB ($6.50) per withdrawal
    • Exchange rate usually better at ATM than airport
  • Download Grab app (ride-sharing, similar to Uber)
    • Essential for getting around Chiang Mai
    • Very affordable taxi and motorbike options
  • Get to your accommodation
    • Should be pre-booked for first 2 weeks
    • Grab is easiest option from airport

First 2-3 Days: Settling In

Take It Slow

Give yourself quiet time to recover from jet lag and adjust to your new environment. The first few days should prioritize rest and basic orientation.

  • Rest and manage jet lag
  • Locate nearby grocery store (Tesco Lotus, Big C, or local markets)
  • Familiarize yourself with neighborhood
  • Visit local convenience store (7-Eleven on many corners)
  • Confirm accommodation for longer-term stay

Suthep Area Overview

Your base location has excellent expat infrastructure:

  • Large English-speaking expat community
  • Multiple grocery stores within walking distance
  • Numerous Western-style restaurants
  • Bangkok Hospital Chiang Mai (24/7 English-speaking medical)
  • 30+ AA meetings per week in English
  • Gyms, pools, and recreational facilities

60-Day Visa Exemption Timeline

Day 1 (Aug 25): Arrive in Chiang Mai, automatic 60-day stamp
Days 2-3: Settle in, handle jet lag, basic orientation
Days 4-22: Begin next phase (Initial Settling In, Sept 13)
Day 60 (Oct 24): 60-day visa exemption expires
Optional (Oct 19-20): Extend for additional 30 days (total 90 days)
