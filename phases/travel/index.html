Entry Requirements & Visa Exemption

✓ Visa Exemption

US citizens receive a 60-day visa exemption stamp automatically upon arrival at the airport. No advance visa application required.

Documents Needed

  • Valid US passport (6+ months validity)
  • Return or onward ticket (airline may ask)
  • Proof of accommodation

⚠ Important Note

You cannot apply for the DTV visa from inside Thailand. This 60-day exemption is your entry window. You'll apply for the 5-year DTV visa later from Vietnam.

Airport Arrivals: First Night Checklist

Travel Tips & Considerations

Layover Strategy

With 13+ hours in Seoul, you might consider a short layover hotel to rest. Or stay in the airport terminal for a quieter transition.

Medication & Health

Keep all medications in original containers in your carry-on bag. Bring your 90-day medication supply as planned. Medical records should also be accessible.

Flight Comfort

Pack compression socks, neck pillow, and stay hydrated during the long journey. Move around the cabin when possible to maintain circulation.

Connection Tips

Allow plenty of time between connections. Ask airline staff about connecting flight status. International transfers typically need 2-3 hours minimum.

Luggage Tracking

Tag all luggage with your new Thailand contact info (phone number or temporary accommodation address). Take photos of luggage tags before checking bags.

Airport Arrival Time

Chiang Mai airport is smaller and less crowded than Bangkok. Immigration should be relatively quick. Budget 45 minutes to 1 hour for arrival procedures.
