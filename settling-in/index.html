Initial Settling In

Your first 3 weeks in Chiang Mai: establishing essential services and building community connections

Timeline: August 26 - September 16, 2025

Duration: 3 weeks

First 3 Quiet Days

  • Visit nearby grocery store to familiarize yourself with the area
  • Download and set up Grab app for transportation (taxis & motorbikes)
  • Rest and adjust to the time zone

Set Up Basic Services

  • Cleaner: 500-800 THB ($15-24) for 2-3 hours
  • Primary Care Doctor: Recommended: Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic
  • Physical Therapist: Essential for your medical plan
  • Pharmacy: Locate nearby for prescription refills

Connect with Recovery Community

  • Attend nearby AA meetings (50+ per week in Chiang Mai)
  • Buddhist recovery or Refuge Recovery meetings
  • Find a sponsor
  • Research addiction counseling options:
    • Counselling Thailand
    • The Cabin alumni network

Key Information

Recovery Resources in Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai has a well-established recovery community with 50+ AA meetings per week. The city is known as a hub for expats seeking recovery and wellness. Many meetings are English-speaking or have English translation available.

Cost of Living Notes

  • Housing in Suthep: $250-500/month
  • Physical therapy: $25-75/session (vs $100+ in US)
  • ATM withdrawals: 220 THB ($6.50) fee per transaction
  • Western-style restaurants: Abundant in Chiang Mai

Transportation

Grab is the primary app for taxis and motorbikes in Chiang Mai. Prices are very inexpensive compared to Western countries. The Suthep area has good access to all essential services.

What Comes Next

After establishing your routine and services (by mid-September), you'll move on to the next phase: Getting a Medical Plan required for your DTV visa application.

This medical consultation with a Thai doctor creates the foundation for visa approval and should show that you need 6+ months of ongoing treatment (physical therapy/pain management).
